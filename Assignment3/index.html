The Hobbit: An Unexpected Journey

A film directed by Peter Jackson

Inspired by John Ronald Tolkien Novel(s)

Act 1

The start of the story begins with meeting one of our main characters, Bilbo. We also get a glimpse into his home, the Shire. Gandalf makes an unexpected visit to Bilbo as well as the 13 dwarves that will make up the Thorin Oakenshield Company. Lastly, Bilbo is offered the proposition of an adventure, which after some begrudgement he accepts and the journey begins.

Act 2

This is the large part of the journey and as an almost three hour movie, some notable moments include:

  • Encounter with the trolls which almost results in the company being eaten. Bilbo finds the sword Sting.
  • The company travels to Rivendell, an Elvish city, where secrets about the Lonely Mountain are revealed.
  • Traveling through the mountains during a battle between stone giants.
  • The group is captured by goblins and Bilbo becomes separated from the company.
  • Bilbo encounters Gollum and obtains the Ring.
  • Bilbo and the company escape.

Act 3

After Bilbo rejoins the company they are attacked by Orcs and chased into the trees at the edge of a cliff. Thorin comes close to death and Bilbo shows his bravery by trying to protect him. After Gandalf’s call for help, they are saved by a group of eagles. The film ends with the company brought to safety on a mountain peak where they look toward the Lonely Mountain in the distance.
