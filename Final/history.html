History of Black Cats

Black cats have had a long and complex history shaped by culture, superstition, and symbolism. In ancient Egypt, cats were highly respected and even worshipped, often associated with protection and good fortune. However, during the Middle Ages in Europe, black cats became linked to witchcraft and were believed to be companions of witches, which led to widespread fear and persecution.

Despite these negative associations, black cats are seen as symbols of luck and prosperity in many parts of the world. In countries like Japan and the United Kingdom, they are often considered good omens, especially for love and success. Today, black cats remain iconic and mysterious, though they are sometimes overlooked in shelters due to lingering superstitions.
